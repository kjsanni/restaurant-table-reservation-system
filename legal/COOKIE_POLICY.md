# Cookie Policy

**Last updated:** 20 July 2026

## 1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit the Restaurant Table Reservation System ("RTRS"). Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, and pixels. This Cookie Policy explains what we use and why.

## 2. How we use cookies

RTRS uses the following categories of cookies:

### Strictly necessary (always active)
These are required for the Service to function and cannot be disabled.
- **Session & authentication tokens (JWT)** — keep you logged in and secure your session.
- **CSRF tokens** — protect against cross-site request forgery.
- **Load-balancing / routing** — direct your requests to the correct server instance.

### Functional / preference
- **UI preferences** — sidebar state, theme, and language choices stored in local storage.
- **Tenant context** — remembers the active restaurant/tenant for multi-tenant accounts.

### Analytics / performance
- We use privacy-respecting usage metrics to operate and improve RTRS. These do not advertise to you and are processed under our legitimate interests.

We do **not** currently use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

## 3. Managing cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Blocking strictly necessary cookies may break core functionality (e.g. you will be logged out). For preference cookies, you can clear local storage in your browser.

## 4. Changes

We may update this policy as our use of cookies evolves. The "Last updated" date reflects the latest revision.
