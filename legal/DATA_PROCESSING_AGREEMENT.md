# Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**Last updated:** 20 July 2026

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of, and is incorporated into, the [Terms of Service](./TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md) between **Vibespot Technologies Ltd.** ("Processor", "we"), a company incorporated in the **Republic of Ghana**, and each restaurant or organisation using the Restaurant Table Reservation System ("RTRS") as a Tenant ("Controller", "you", "Merchant").

Where a Tenant uses RTRS to process personal data of its guests and staff, Vibespot Technologies Ltd. acts as a processor on the Controller's behalf.

## 1. Definitions

Capitalised terms not defined here have the meaning given in the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) of Ghana and, where relevant, the GDPR. "Personal Data", "Processing", "Data Subject", "Controller", "Processor", and "Sub-processor" have the corresponding meanings.

## 2. Scope & roles

- The Controller determines the purposes and means of processing the personal data it enters into RTRS (guest reservations, customer profiles, staff records).
- The Processor provides the RTRS platform and Processes that Personal Data only on the Controller's documented instructions.
- For platform-level data (the Controller's own account, billing, and authentication data), the Processor acts as an independent controller under its [Privacy Policy](./PRIVACY_POLICY.md).

## 3. Processor obligations

The Processor shall:
- (a) Process Personal Data only on the Controller's documented instructions, including with regard to international transfers, unless required by law.
- (b) Ensure persons authorised to Process the data are bound by confidentiality.
- (c) Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures (see [GDPR Compliance Statement](./GDPR_COMPLIANCE.md) and our Privacy Policy).
- (d) Assist the Controller via appropriate measures with data-subject requests and security, breach-notice, and assessment obligations.
- (e) Make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits as reasonably necessary.
- (f) At the Controller's choice, delete or return Personal Data on termination of the Service, unless retention is required by law.

## 4. Controller obligations

The Controller shall:
- (a) Ensure it has a lawful basis for the Personal Data it submits to RTRS and that it complies with the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) of Ghana.
- (b) Provide its guests and staff with a privacy notice and honour their rights.
- (c) Issue only lawful, documented instructions to the Processor.

## 5. Sub-processing

The Controller grants general written authorisation for the Processor to engage Sub-processors (hosting, database, cache/queue, email/SMS, payments). The Processor shall impose data-protection obligations on Sub-processors that are no less protective than those in this DPA and remains liable for their performance.

## 6. International transfers

Where Personal Data is transferred outside Ghana, the Processor shall apply appropriate safeguards consistent with the Data Protection Act, 2012, and, for EEA/UK data, Standard Contractual Clauses or a UK IDTA.

## 7. Security breach

The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data breach affecting the Controller's data, providing sufficient information to enable the Controller to meet its own breach-notification duties (including to the Data Protection Commission of Ghana where required).

## 8. Governing law

This DPA is governed by the laws of the **Republic of Ghana**.
